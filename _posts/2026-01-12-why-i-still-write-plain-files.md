I've tried most of the popular note apps. They're good — genuinely. But every couple of years I drift back to a folder of plain `.md` files, and I've stopped fighting the pull.

## Durability beats features

A Markdown file written in 2014 still opens today, in any editor, on any machine, with no account and no sync conflict. That's a feature no app can match, because it isn't a feature — it's the *absence* of one.

When the tool is just text, the tool can't break. It can't get acquired, sunset, or decide my notes now require a subscription.

## Friction is the point

Plain files make some things harder: no backlinks out of the box, no nice mobile capture, no search across a graph. But that friction forces me to keep a structure I actually understand. I know where things are because I had to decide where to put them.

## My whole setup

- A `notes/` folder synced with git
- One file per idea, dated in the filename
- Ripgrep for search
- This blog, which is just a subset of that folder

That's it. It has outlived three apps and counting.
