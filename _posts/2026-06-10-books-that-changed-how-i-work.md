Plenty of books are good. Very few change what you *do* on a Tuesday. These three did.

## *The Pragmatic Programmer*

The idea that stuck: **don't live with broken windows.** Small, tolerated messes signal that mess is acceptable, and decay accelerates. I now fix the tiny annoyance — the misleading variable name, the stale comment — while I'm already in the file. It compounds.

## *Thinking in Systems* by Donella Meadows

The idea that stuck: **the goal of a system is what it actually does, not what it says it wants.** When a process keeps producing a bad outcome, the outcome *is* the goal, structurally speaking. Stop blaming the people inside it and look at the incentives.

## *Four Thousand Weeks* by Oliver Burkeman

The idea that stuck: **you will never clear the backlog, so choose on purpose.** Productivity systems sell the fantasy of getting on top of everything. You won't. The skill is deciding what to neglect, deliberately, instead of letting the pile decide for you.

---

None of these are new, and that's the point. The ideas had to be lived in, not just read, before they took.
